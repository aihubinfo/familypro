If you are planning to buy YouTube Premium, comparing prices too early can be misleading.  
A better order is: understand plan types and feature differences first, then compare country prices, and finally make a purchase decision based on your own usage pattern.

> Data date: 2026-04-01  
> Note: Prices may change based on exchange rates, taxes, regional policy, and account history. Figures below are for reference only. Final pricing is determined at checkout.

## 1) What YouTube Premium Plans Are Available

YouTube Premium commonly appears in three plan types (availability varies by region):

- Individual plan
- Family plan
- Student plan (requires student verification)

### 1.1 Feature differences between plan types

Shared benefits across these plans usually include:

- Ad-free YouTube playback
- Background play and picture-in-picture
- Offline downloads
- Access to YouTube Music Premium

The main differences are user count and eligibility:

- Individual: one account, simplest to activate.
- Family: usually supports multiple members (limit depends on region rules).
- Student: often lower price, but requires valid student status and may require periodic re-verification.

### 1.2 What people often miss before buying

- Student plans are not available in every market.
- Family plans can have region and family-group history restrictions.
- Individual plans are the most stable, but may not be the lowest long-term cost.

## 3) Buying Advice by Usage Scenario

### 3.1 You use YouTube heavily as a single user

- Compare 3-month and 12-month totals instead of only one-month price.
- If usage is stable, long-cycle budgeting gives a clearer decision basis.

### 3.2 You have 2-5 stable members

- Evaluate family plans by per-person effective cost.
- Confirm member eligibility and regional rules before paying.

### 3.3 You qualify for student verification

- Student plans can be very cost-effective, but verify local availability and renewal requirements.
- If verification may expire soon, estimate post-expiry cost in advance.

## 4) Conclusion

If your goal is to balance stable usage and cost control, complete plan matching first and then compare same-period total cost before placing an order.  
Decide your scenario first (single user, shared family usage, or student eligibility), then pick the plan with the best total-cost fit.
